Blog and Reflection

Empowering Through ICT Education: My Journey as a Tech Mentor

As an aspiring IT educator and tech mentor, my journey has been defined by a passion for simplifying technology and empowering others with digital skills. From my early education in Kenya to my professional experiences and ongoing pursuit of knowledge, every step has shaped my mission to make technology accessible and impactful. This reflection explores my academic path, professional milestones, certifications, skills, and aspirations, highlighting my commitment to fostering digital literacy and innovation.

Academic Journey

My educational foundation began at Nyanguuu Junior Academy, where I completed my Kenya Certificate of Primary Education in 2013. This early milestone sparked my curiosity and set the stage for my academic pursuits. At Wanglapala High School, I earned my Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education in 2017. The challenges I faced during this period taught me resilience and the importance of perseverance, qualities that continue to guide my professional ethos.

In 2018, I embarked on my technical education at Kababu Youth Polytechnic, where I completed a course in Computer Packages. This introduction to computing ignited my passion for I.C.T. and inspired me to dive deeper into the field. From 2018 to 2021, I pursued a Certificate in Information Communication Technology at the East African Institute of Certified Studies. This program provided a solid grounding in hardware basics, software applications, and system operations, fueling my enthusiasm for technology.

In 2022, I enrolled in a Diploma in Information Technology at KCA University, completing it in 2024. This program equipped me with advanced skills in software configuration, system maintenance, network administration, and troubleshooting. Concurrently, I completed a course in Web Design and Development at the African Institute of Research and Development Studies from January to April 2022. This training enhanced my ability to create user-friendly, functional websites, adding a creative dimension to my technical expertise. Additionally, in 2021, I earned a driving certification from Imperial Driving School, which has enhanced my mobility and responsiveness in professional settings.

Looking ahead, I am excited to begin a Diploma in Teacher Trainer Education at the Kenya School of TVET in January 2025. This program will enable me to blend my technical expertise with my passion for teaching, equipping me to train and inspire the next generation of I.C.T. professionals. My academic journey has been a blend of technical skill-building and personal growth, preparing me to make a meaningful impact in the I.C.T. and education sectors.

Professional Experience

My most formative professional experience was my three-month tenure as an I.C.T. Technician at the National Health Insurance Fund (NHIF) in Kisumu from January to March 2024. Working in a fast-paced environment, I provided hands-on technical support, troubleshooting hardware and software issues for desktops, laptops, and peripherals. I assisted staff via phone, email, and in-person, ensuring minimal downtime and optimal system performance. This role honed my ability to work under pressure, solve complex technical challenges, and maintain accurate records of technical procedures.

At NHIF, I collaborated with my supervisor, Mr. Seka Fredrick, and a team of professionals, learning the value of clear communication and teamwork in delivering high-quality technical support. The experience exposed me to real-world I.C.T. challenges, such as diagnosing system errors and configuring software to meet organizational needs. I also implemented preventative maintenance strategies to enhance system efficiency, reducing technical disruptions. This role solidified my commitment to a career in I.C.T. and inspired me to pursue opportunities that combine technical expertise with mentorship.

My certifications reflect my dedication to building a strong foundation in I.C.T. and education. I hold a Certificate in Information Communication Technology from the East African Institute of Certified Studies, which provided me with essential technical skills. My Web Design and Development certification from the African Institute of Research and Development Studies equipped me with the ability to create functional and user-centric websites. Additionally, my Diploma in Information Technology from KCA University enhanced my expertise in system administration and troubleshooting. Looking forward, I am eager to earn my Diploma in Teacher Trainer Education from the Kenya School of TVET, which will further my ability to educate and mentor others in I.C.T.

My achievements extend beyond certifications. I have developed I.C.T. lesson plans for diploma and certificate students, designed to make complex concepts accessible and engaging. I have created “Tech Simplified” video content for platforms like X and Facebook, aimed at demystifying technology for diverse audiences. My web design samples showcase my ability to create user-centric digital solutions, and through mentorship, I have helped students master practical digital skills, fostering their confidence and competence in I.C.T.
